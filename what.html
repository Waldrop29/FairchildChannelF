What is the Fairchild Channel F?

The Fairchild Channel F is a home video game console released in 1976 by Fairchild Semiconductor. It was the first console to use interchangeable cartridges, allowing players to switch games easily. The Channel F featured a unique design with a built-in controller and was known for its innovative technology during the early days of home gaming.

The Fairchild Channel F is the Wii console of its time. It was popular from 1976 to 1977.
