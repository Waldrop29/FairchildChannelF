Jerry Lawson

Jerry Lawson was the first black engineer in the video game industry.

He was born on December 1, 1940, in Brooklyn, New York. Lawson developed an early interest in electronics and went on to study engineering. In the early 1970s, he joined Fairchild Semiconductor, where he led the team that developed the Fairchild Channel F, the first home video game console to use interchangeable cartridges. This innovation revolutionized the gaming industry and paved the way for future consoles.

Lawson's contributions to the video game industry were significant, and he is often credited with helping to shape the future of gaming. He continued to work in the technology field throughout his career, advocating for diversity and inclusion in STEM fields. Jerry Lawson passed away on April 9, 2011, but his legacy lives on through his groundbreaking work in the video game industry.
